Project Objective

Using WebGL Import and render an animated textured 3D model. Then to prove proficeny in WEBGL rendering, spice up the scene the object is included in with lighting, a cubemap skybox, and reflections.

User Input
You can move the magnemite and various other aspects of the Scene per the keys indicated below.

Magnemite Movement keys
‘w’ - move magnemite to his left (intially away from camera)
‘a’- move magnemite backward (intially left from camera)
‘s’- move magnemite to his right (intially toward from camera)
‘d’- move magnemite forward (intially right from camera)
Camera Movement keys
‘r’- roll camera clockwise
‘R’- roll camera counter clockwise
‘y’- yaw camera clockwise
‘Y’- yaw camera counter clockwise
‘p’- pitch camera clockwise
‘P’- pitch camera counter clockwise
Light Movement keys
‘1’ - move light up globally
‘2’- move light down globally
‘3’- move light left globally
‘4’- move light right globally
‘5’- move light forward globally
‘6’- move light backward globally
Transform keys
Because I found it helpful for examining the scene. You can also change from rotating the camera locally (around it's center) to rotating around 0,0.
To do this press "G" and then the roll, pitch and yaw keys will then have changed functionality.

‘g’- turn off global transformations for Camera
‘G’- turn on global transformations for Camera
Light Enabling Keys
‘l’- turn light sphere off
‘L’- turn light sphere on

Key Features

3D object rendering
Using X, Y and Z coordinates we can create vertices which can be connected via designated triangles. These vertices and polygons result in our final image.
Lighting
The scene above uses the basic calculations for Diffuse, Ambient, and Specular lighting. This results in a primitive implementation of the way lighting works in real life. To achieve this, we have a light source represented by a little white sphere. Then based on the location of this one light source we can change the color of various fragments of our model to end up with our final lit model.
Animation
The whole model moves on a Parametric equation that is essentially just a parabola that goes back and forth to emulate what I would describe as a levitating bounciness. The magnets just rotate. Together this shows the hierarchical transformations as the whole model is moving the magnets have two layers of transformations applied to them.
Skybox and Reflections
We have a cube map which we use to both create a skybox and map reflections onto on object as described in the linked Wikipedia page. This is one of the many ways to emulate sky and reflections as discovered by artists and computer scientists and while being one of the simplest of them computationally still ends up with a good reflection effect on single objects like our above scene.
Texture
Using texture mapping of vertices to an image containing the desired textures we can color the vertices of our 3D model which results in the model have the colored pattern we want on our final model.
